Dual Special Needs Plans (D-SNP)

What are they are?

D-SNP Plans are a type of Medicare Advantage plan designed for individuals who qualify for both Medicare and Medicaid ("dual-eligible"). These plans provide specialized, coordinated care, combining Part A, Part B, and prescription drug coverage (Part D) into one, often with $0 premiums and enhanced benefits like dental, vision, and transportation.

Key Details About D-SNP Plans:

Eligibility: Must have both Medicare and Medicaid (Medi-Cal in California)

May include both Vision & Dental Coverage for both routine and comprehensive care

Benefits: Often include extra services not covered by original Medicare, such as over-the-counter (OTC) items, dental, vision, hearing, and healthy food/produce allowances

Care Coordination: Focuses on integrating care between Medicare and Medicaid to improve health outcomes and manage chronic conditions. Cost: Generally, these plans feature $0 or very low monthly premiums and low cost-sharing for prescriptions

Enrollment: While some plans allow for passive enrollment, others require active enrollment through Medicare or the provider

D-SNPs are a popular choice for eligible, low-income seniors or people with disabilities, with nearly 3 in 10 dual-eligible individuals enrolled in 2021
