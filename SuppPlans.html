Medicare Supplement Plans

Also known as MEDIGAP Plans

Key Offerings and Coverage

Medigap plans are standardized by the government and sold by private insurers to cover specific cost gaps:

Medicare Part A Coinsurance and Hospital Costs: Covers up to 365 extra days after Medicare benefits are used.

Medicare Part B Coinsurance or Copayment: Covers the 20% coinsurance for doctor bills and services.

Blood Coverage: Covers the first 3 pints of blood.

Hospice Care Coinsurance: Covers Part A hospice costs.

Skilled Nursing Facility Coinsurance: Covers this cost.

Part A Deductible: Helps pay the inpatient hospital deductible.

Part B Excess Charges: Covers charges if a doctor doesn't accept assignment.

Foreign Travel Emergency: Covers up to 80% of emergency medical care outside the U.S. (within plan limits).

What Medigap Plans Do Not Cover

These plans generally do not include:

  • Long-term care (custodial care)
  • Vision or Dental Coverage
  • Hearing aids & Eyeglasses
  • Private-duty nursing
  • Prescription drugs (must purchase a Part D plan separately)

Common Plan Options

Plan G: Currently one of the most comprehensive options, covering all costs except for the Part B deductible.

Plan N: Covers 100% of Part B coinsurance, but requires copayments for some office and emergency room visits.

High-Deductible Plans (G or F): Lower monthly premiums, but you pay a high deductible before coverage kicks in.

Key Considerations

Enrollment: The best time to buy is during your six-month Medigap Open Enrollment Period, when insurers cannot deny coverage or charge more due to health conditions.

Network: You can see any provider who accepts Medicare.

Consistency: Plans are standardized, meaning a Plan G from Company A has the same benefits as a Plan G from Company B, though premiums may vary.
